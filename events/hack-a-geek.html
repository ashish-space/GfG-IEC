About Hack-A-Geek 2026

Are you a coding wizard? A master of AI prompting? Or just a student with a brilliant idea and the grit to make it happen? This hackathon is for you.

We believe great innovation shouldn't be gated by technical barriers. Whether you write every line of Python from scratch or leverage AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or GitHub Copilot — we want to see your creativity in action. This is a level playing field where the solution and the impact matter most.

Organized by the GeeksforGeeks Student Chapter at IEC College of Engineering & Technology, Hack-A-Geek 2026 is an offline hackathon spanning multiple colleges across Gautam Buddha Nagar, Uttar Pradesh, India.

🤖

Use ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot — AI is your co-pilot. Results matter more than the tool.

🌐

Open to All Students

Product Design Focus

📜

📌 Eligibility: Open to all — Undergraduate, Postgraduate, Engineering students. No tech barrier: code, AI tools, or no-code all welcome. Individual participation only.

Rules & Guidelines

  • 🛠️
    Bring Your Own Tools: You are encouraged to use AI assistants (LLMs, code generators) to speed up your workflow.
  • 💡
    Originality First: While AI is your co-pilot, the final logic, integration, and "Big Idea" must be your own. Plagiarism of existing projects is strictly prohibited.
  • ⚖️
    Ethics First: Any AI-generated content must be reviewed by you to ensure accuracy and ethical standards before submission.
  • 📤
    Project Submission: Submit your project link (GitHub / Drive) + a brief video demo of your solution within the deadline on 28 Mar 2026.
  • 📝
    Idea Submission: Create a post on TheWide explaining your idea and share a screenshot as your submission (24 Mar – 27 Mar 2026).
  • 📱
    GfG Connect Post: After the hackathon, post on GfG Connect explaining how you built your project (28 Mar – 30 Mar 2026).
  • 👤
    Individual Only: This is a solo hackathon. Each participant competes independently — no team submissions.
  • 🏫
    Offline Presence Required: The offline round on 28 March requires physical presence at the venue in Gautam Buddha Nagar, UP.

Judging Criteria

Each submission is evaluated equally across four key parameters:

25%
💡 Innovation
How unique and original is the idea? Does it stand out from typical solutions?
25%
🎯 Problem-Solution Fit
Does your solution actually and effectively solve the stated problem?
25%
⚙️ Technical Execution
How well did you use your tools (Code / AI) to build a working, functional prototype?
25%
✨ User Experience
Is the solution easy to use, well-presented, and visually intuitive?
